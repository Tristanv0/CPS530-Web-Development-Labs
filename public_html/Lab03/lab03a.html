History of Kitakyushu-Fukuoka

Kitakyushu-Fukuoka, a vibrant metropolitan area of Japan, is known for its rich history and culturial heritage. Historically, Kitakyushu was a major pillar of Japan's economy, and Fukuoka was a key harbor city, cultivating cultural exchanges. In 1963, the Kitakyushu-Fukuoka Consolidation occurred, combining two smaller municipalities into a large city. This improved administrative efficiency, allowed leveraging resources more effectively, and ultimately promoting regional growth and stability.

Geographic Location

Kitakyushu-Fukuoka is located in the southwestern part of Japan, on the island of Kyushu.

Population

Although Kitakyushu-Fukuoka is the 80th most populated city in the world, it appears the population has started to follow a decline trend. The U.N. Also projects that the population in this city will decrease further.

Kokura Castle



A historic castle in Kitakyushi with a beautiful garden and museum inside.

Mojiko Retro


A district in Kitakyushu known for its well-preserved historic buildings from the Meiji and Taisho eras.

A historic castle in Fukuoka, though mostly reconstructed, offers beautiful views of the city from the top.
